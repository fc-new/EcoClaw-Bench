## Prompt

Context: Generalized (or weakened) quantum measurements introduce qualitatively new features in fundamental quantum physics that do not appear with simple projective measurements. Moreover, sequences of weak measurements can be made effectively continuous, resulting in quantum state evolutions described by quantum stochastic processes. Crucially, continuous quantum monitoring makes feedback control feasible, allowing parameters in the system Hamiltonian to be dynamically adjusted based on measurement outcomes.

In classical stochastic systems, an important class of problems involves rare-event physics, specifically processes characterized by unlikely transitions that start and end at specific points in phase space. In classical contexts, these rare events can be analyzed by introducing a canonical phase space formulation and identifying optimal paths by minimizing a suitable action functional under specified boundary conditions.

A comparable situation emerges in continuously measured quantum systems, where one seeks to prepare an initial quantum state and later observes a rare final (postselected) state at a specified time.

Here, we would like to develop a stochastic path integral formulation of continuous quantum measurements, deriving an action principle that naturally incorporates boundary conditions such as postselection. This formulation complements and generalizes the known stochastic Schrödinger or master equation approaches, recovering them as special cases when postselection is absent.

Path-integral treatments of continuous quantum measurement have a long history, notably including Mensky's work on restricted Feynman path integrals from over three decades ago. Our approach differs fundamentally from previous formulations by constructing a probability distribution for trajectories through a canonically doubled (potentially mixed) quantum state space.

By doubling the state space to form a canonical quantum phase space, we can extremize the resulting stochastic action. This procedure identifies the most probable trajectories connecting specified preselected and postselected states. As a paradigmatic demonstration, we would like to apply our formalism to continuously monitored solid-state qubits, identifying their most likely dynamics in both quantum demolition and non-demolition measurement regimes. We also aim to further characterize rare quantum jump events occurring within the quantum Zeno regime.
Question: The objective is to develop a stochastic path integral framework for continuous quantum measurement, enabling the derivation of an action principle suitable for analyzing rare quantum events with defined boundary conditions, such as preselected and postselected states. This method should generalize existing stochastic Schrödinger or master equation formulations, explicitly recovering them when no postselection is applied.

4. **Demonstrate** the developed formalism explicitly by analyzing a continuously monitored solid-state qubit, focusing on both quantum demolition and non-demolition measurement regimes.
5. **Characterize** the structure and dynamics of rare quantum jump events within the quantum Zeno measurement regime using this new quantum phase-space representation.
